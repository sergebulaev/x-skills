# Worked Humanizer Examples

## Example 1 - Single tweet, AI vocab + em dash

**Before:**
> In today's fast-paced world, founders must leverage AI tools to fundamentally
> streamline their workflow — it's a game-changer.

Tells: "in today's fast-paced world", leverage, fundamentally, streamline, em
dash, game-changer, zero specifics, no first line that lands alone.

**After:**
> Most founders use AI to write faster.
>
> The ones who win use it to decide faster. Same tools, different job.

Why it is better: a complete one-liner contrarian (X1) take, lands in the first
line, no AI vocab, no em dash, one clear idea, reads in a glance.

## Example 2 - Tweet over 280 from emoji

**Before** (looks like 276 display chars, but 4 emoji = 8 chars, so 284):
> shipping season is here 🚀🚀 we cut our deploy time from 40 min to 6 min and
> honestly it changed how the whole team works 🔥🔥 here is the one change that
> did most of it and why it matters for you

Tells: over 280 once emoji are counted as 2 each, emoji spam, two ideas, weak
trailing hook.

**After** (single tweet, under 280):
> we cut deploy time from 40 min to 6 min.
>
> one change did most of it: we stopped rebuilding the whole image on every
> push. here is how.

Or escalate to a thread if the "how" needs real steps (use x-thread-builder).

## Example 3 - Thread opener that closes its own loop

**Before (tweet 1):**
> Here are 5 ways to grow on X: post consistently, engage with others, write
> threads, use good hooks, and be authentic.

Tells: the whole payoff is in tweet 1, so no one expands; vague items; rule of
five with no specifics.

**After (tweet 1):**
> I grew from 200 to 11,000 followers on X in 7 months.
>
> 5 things did most of it. Number 3 is the one nobody talks about.

Now the loop is open (what is number 3?), tweet 1 promises a real result, and
the body has room to teach each item with a concrete example.

## Example 4 - Negative parallelism

**Before:**
> It's not about posting more. It's about posting better.

**After:**
> Posting more did nothing for me. Posting one sharp thing a day did.

Direct claim, first-person, a specific contrast instead of the AI "not X, it's
Y" rhythm.

## What to preserve

- The user's real claim and numbers.
- Lowercase-casual voice if that is their register.
- `..` soft pauses.
- Their actual story. Never invent a detail to make a tweet land.
