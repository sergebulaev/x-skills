# Worked Teardowns

## Example 1 - Single tweet (X1 One-Liner Contrarian)

**Source (pasted by user):**
> Most people optimize their resume. The ones who get hired optimize their
> network six months before they need it.

**Teardown:**
- **Container:** single tweet.
- **Formula:** X1 One-Liner Contrarian (confidence high).
- **Structure:** line 1 names the common behavior ("optimize their resume");
  line 2 flips it with a specific, defensible contrarian move ("optimize their
  network six months before"). The specificity ("six months") makes it
  repostable instead of a platitude.
- **Primary goal:** reposts. A complete, quotable opinion travels because
  reposting it signals the reader's own taste.
- **Why it worked:** no setup, no thread, lands in the first line, and the claim
  is true enough to defend in the replies.
- **Source audit:** clean. No em dashes, no AI vocab, no hashtags.

**Blank template for your topic:**
```
Most {group} {common behavior}.

The ones who {win} {contrarian move with a specific detail}.
```

## Example 2 - Thread (X7 Listicle-Thread Promise)

**Source (tweet 1, pasted by user):**
> I have reviewed 400+ landing pages this year.
>
> 9 mistakes show up on almost all of them. Number 4 quietly kills the most
> signups.

**Teardown:**
- **Container:** thread.
- **Formula:** X7 Listicle-Thread Promise (confidence high).
- **Structure:** tweet 1 stacks an authority number ("400+"), a promise ("9
  mistakes"), and an open loop ("number 4 quietly kills the most"). The body
  delivers one mistake per tweet. The reader expands to find number 4.
- **Primary goal:** bookmarks. A teaching thread with concrete examples is
  save-bait; readers keep the whole chain.
- **Why it worked:** the authority number earns trust, the open loop earns the
  tap, and front-loading the strongest mistakes keeps tap-through high.
- **Source audit:** clean opener. Check the body tweets for an external link
  (move any to a reply) and uniform length.

**Blank template for your topic:**
```
I have {authority action with a number} this year.

{N} {mistakes/lessons} show up on almost all of them. Number {k} {open-loop consequence}.

---

1. {Mistake name}

{2-3 lines that teach it, with a concrete example.}
```

## Example 3 - Quote tweet (X4 Value-Add)

**Source:** a quote tweet of a "just ship it" take, adding:
> True, but "ship it" without a way to measure whether it worked is just
> gambling with extra steps. Ship it AND instrument it.

**Teardown:**
- **Container:** quote tweet.
- **Formula:** X4 Quote-Tweet Value-Add (confidence high).
- **Structure:** concedes the original ("True, but"), then adds the missing
  nuance (measurement), then lands a memorable reframe ("gambling with extra
  steps").
- **Primary goal:** reposts. The quote borrows the original's reach and the
  added nuance gives the reader a reason to repost this version.
- **Why it worked:** added value, did not dunk. The reframe line is the
  screenshot.
- **Source audit:** clean.

**Blank template:**
```
{Concede the quoted take in 3-5 words}, but {the missing nuance}.

{Memorable reframe line.}
```
